Nasturtium Pumpkin Seed Bread

Ingredients:

500g (1lb) flour
2 tsp salt
2 tsp fast action yeast
2 tbsp olive oil
300ml (½pint) of warm water
75g (3 oz) pumpkin seeds
10 nasturtium flowers (more for bright flecks through the bread)
1 tbsp fresh nasturtium seeds, chopped

Preparation

Mix all the dry ingredients into a bowl.
Make a well in the middle and pour in the oil and the water.
Draw the flour into the centre and mix well until the dough leaves the sides of the bowl.
Once the flour and liquids are combined, quickly work in the petals and seeds.
(If you want the petals to look large and more complete then add them towards the end of the kneading process so they don’t break up so much.)
Turn the dough onto a floured surface and knead it for five minutes.
Shape the dough and put it into a lightly greased 1kg/2lb loaf tin.
Cover loosely with lightly oiled greased cling film and let it rise in a moderately warm place for about an hour, by which time it should have doubled in size.
Bake for about 35 to 40 minutes in an oven pre-heated to 400°F (200°C).
Turn the loaf onto a wire rack and cool completely before slicing – if you can bear to wait.
